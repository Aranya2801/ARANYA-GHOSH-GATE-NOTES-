How to Use These Notes

01
First Pass
Read the full notes for each topic. Derive every formula at least once. Build the mental model — don't skip this step.
02
Active Practice
Attempt every practice problem without hints first. Check solutions only after a genuine attempt. The struggle is the learning.
03
Spaced Revision
Use the formula sheets weekly. Don't re-read — test yourself with blank-paper recall before checking the sheet.
04
PYQ Analysis
After each topic, solve 5+ years of PYQs. When you get one wrong, trace back to which concept you missed — don't just note the answer.
05
Weak Area Loops
Keep a personal error log. Every wrong answer has a root cause. Fix the root, not the symptom. Return to that topic in two weeks.
06
Exam Week
Formula sheets only. No new topics. High-confidence problems only. Simulate full mock tests under timed conditions.

About This Project

This repository is the exact study system I built for GATE 2026. It started as my personal notes — disorganized, incomplete, full of margins and shortcuts. Over six months of preparation, it became something more deliberate: a structured thinking companion designed to build genuine understanding, not just exam-ready recall.

The organizing principle is simple: every idea earns its place. Every formula has a derivation. Every algorithm has a worked trace. Every section ends with an honest statement of what you must be able to do — not just what you must know.

I secured AIR 3 in GATE 2026 (Data Science & AI) and AIR 147 in GATE 2026 (CS & IT). I am sharing everything I used — notes, practice problems, PYQ analysis, strategy — because I benefited enormously from materials shared openly by others during my preparation.

What makes these notes different?

Most GATE notes are lists of facts. These are not. Each topic has a conceptual narrative — an explanation of why something is true, not just what is true. The practice problems are selected to reveal the most common reasoning gaps, not to be exhaustive. The summary sheets are designed for the last 10 days of preparation, not for first-pass learning.

I used these notes to prepare in 6 months while juggling college coursework. They are efficient by necessity. Every page justifies its existence.

How to contribute

Found an error? Have a better explanation for a topic? Want to add a solved PYQ? Open a pull request on GitHub. Contributions are welcome and credited. Please follow the note-template format and maintain the tone: precise, honest, without unnecessary filler.
